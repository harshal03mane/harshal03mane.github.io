Harischandra Fort

The fort is quite ancient. Remnants of Microlithic man have been discovered here. The various Puranas (ancient scriptures) like Matsyapurana, Agnipurana and Skandapurana include many references about Harishchandragad. Its origin is said to have been in the 6th century, during the rule of Kalachuri dynasty. The citadel was built during this era. The various caves probably have been carved out in the 11th century. In these caves are idols of Lord Vishnu. Though the cliffs are named Taramati and Rohidas, they are not related to Ayodhya. Great sage Changdev (one who created the epic Tatvasaar), used to meditate here in the 14th century. The caves are from the same period. The various constructions on the fort and those existing the surrounding region point to the existence of diverse cultures here. The carvings on the temples of Nageshwar (in Khireshwar village), in the Harishchandreshwar temple and in the cave of Kedareshwar indicate that the fort belongs to the medieval period, since it is related to mahadeva as a totem of tribes Mahadev Koli. They were controlling the fort before Moguls. Later the fort was under the control of Moguls. The Marathas captured it in 1747.

Sandan Velly

Have you ever been on an adventure trip that excites you and offers a life-thrilling experience? Sandhan Valley Trek is one of the most liberating and challenging tasks that you should try once in a lifetime. It takes you through a marvelous journey that offers you a chance to find the innermost depths of your soul and set yourself free from the fear of height. . The Sandhan Valley is one of the greatest canyons in the splendid setting of Sahyadri Western Ghats. It attracts a large number of adventure seekers. It’s a difficult walk for everyone, but once it is completed, it provides you with a sense of satisfaction. If you are a trek lover then Sandhan Valley is the best place for you! Sandhan Valley is located at Bhandardara near Igatpuri. Sandhan Valley is a carved valley that is dark and small. Between the two high sides of a mountain range, there lies a deep narrow rock-floored valley. There is no way for sunshine to enter. It is known as the shadow valley, but it still looks magnificent. The water-carved valley is about 1.5 kilometres long and 200 feet deep. The Alang, Madan, and Kulang mountains surround the valley known as AMK- rated as the toughest trek in Sahyadri. Apart from trekking, there are a variety of other activities to enjoy here, like rappelling, rock climbing, and, of course, camping. Lighting bonfires and spending the night under the stars with your group of friends will be a once-in-a-lifetime experience. It’s a difficult hike for everyone, but once it is completed, it provides you with a sense of satisfaction.

Jagadamba Temple Tahakari

Jagdamba Temple located in Tahakari Village, 30 KM from Akole. Jagdamba Temple Tahakari is a one of the Hemadpanti structure built in an octagonal form on the bank of the river. Many devotees visit Jagdamba temple Tahakari in Navratri Festival. also Tahakari Temple attracts tourists & archaeologist due to its structure.
Best Time To Visit :- Navratri Festival - Every year in Navratri festival many devotees visit Jagdamba Temple Tahakari to offer their prayers to Goddess Jagdamba Mata. Chaitra Purnima Hindu Month Shravan Adhik Mas

Fofsandi is a small Village/hamlet in Akole Taluka in Ahmednagar District of Maharashtra State, India. It comes under Fopsandi Panchayath. It belongs to Khandesh and Northern Maharashtra region . It belongs to Nashik Division . It is located 112 KM towards west from District head quarters Ahmednagar. 147 KM from State capital Mumbai Fofsandi is surrounded by Sangamner Taluka towards East , Sinnar Taluka towards North , Junnar Taluka towards South , Igatpuri Taluka towards west . Sangamner , Sinnar , Junnar , Nashik are the near by Cities to Fofsandi. Fofsandi is small beautiful village surrounded by mountains. Where day was shorten by hours due to mountains.
